# Turkey Consular Operations: Country Brief

**Prepared for:** Turkish Foreign Ministry Meeting  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Turkey operates one of the world's most complex visa regimes, processing millions of applications annually across 250+ diplomatic missions. The country faces unique challenges as both a destination and transit point for migration flows from Asia, Africa, and the Middle East to Europe.

---

## 1. Turkish Visa Landscape

### Visa Types
- **e-Visa (Tourist/Business):** Online system at evisa.gov.tr for 90+ eligible nationalities
- **Sticker Visa:** Required for work, study, long-term residence, and high-scrutiny nationalities
- **Transit Visa:** For travelers passing through Turkish airports
- **Work Permit Visa:** Requires Ministry of Labour pre-approval
- **Student Visa:** Requires university acceptance letter

### Volume Indicators
- **4.5 million+ foreigners** currently in Turkey (PMM 2024 data)
- **3.1 million Syrians** under temporary protection
- **250+ consulates/embassies** processing applications globally
- **e-Visa system** processes millions of applications annually

| Work | 1-2 years | Ministry approval, employer sponsor |

## 2. High-Fraud Origin Countries & Routes

### Primary Irregular Migration Routes to/through Turkey
1. **Eastern Route:** Afghanistan → Iran → Turkey → EU
2. **Syrian Corridor:** Syria → Turkey (land border)
3. **African Route:** Sub-Saharan Africa → Libya/Egypt → Turkey → EU
4. **Central Asian Route:** Pakistan/Bangladesh → Iran → Turkey

### High-Risk Nationalities (Based on IOM/PMM Data)
- Afghanistan (largest group of irregular arrivals)
- Syria (temporary protection, not visa system)
- Pakistan and Bangladesh (transit migration)
- Iraq (mixed flows)
- African nationals using Turkey as transit

### Common Fraud Patterns
1. **Document Forgery:** Forged bank statements, employment letters
2. **Visa Mills:** Organized networks using identical supporting documents across multiple applications
3. **Identity Fraud:** Altered passports, identity swaps
4. **Fake Sponsors:** Fabricated invitation letters and company endorsements
5. **Judicial Document Fraud:** Forged court documents for asylum claims (recent TikTok/social media networks)

## 3. Consular Pain Points

### Operational Challenges
1. **Volume vs. Scrutiny Trade-off:** High application volumes with limited officer time per case
2. **Fragmented Evidence:** Documents arrive in multiple formats, languages, authenticity levels
3. **Cross-Reference Gaps:** No unified system to detect same-document reuse across consulates
4. **Policy Lag:** Diplomatic changes (visa restrictions, bilateral agreements) slow to propagate
5. **Audit Trail Gaps:** Decisions difficult to trace back through evidence chain

### Technology Gaps
- Legacy systems in many consulates
- Limited inter-consulate data sharing
- Manual document verification
- No centralized fraud pattern detection
- Paper-based audit processes
